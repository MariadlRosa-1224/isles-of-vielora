- ## Chapter 1: The stars
  
  At the start of time, there was only darkness. A void where only silence lived no matter where or how far you wanted to go, a nothingness that would remain for eternity. Or that was what it seemed
  
  No one knows how it started, but light was born from the darkness. Small, but bright, so bright its sole presence could light up the whole universe, giving it its first breath.
  
  It was an explosion. The biggest anything would never match ever again. From its energy, the first beings of the universe were born. The first conscious beings that ever existed.
  
  They were powerful, beings of pure mana capable of godly feast, each governing over a concept of reality they could manipulate as they wished.
  
  They were known as gods, said to be represented by each star that exists in the universe, small or big. They with their infinite power shaped the universe in their ways creating countless worlds to govern, each one completely different from any other with creatures made in their image, that would worship them from eternity.
  
  Here is where we put focus on our small planet.
- ## Chapter 2: Humanity
  
  The world before the fall has been lost as years passed, how it was formed, the history of its civilizations, everything is as gone, as earth is, scattered and with only their ruins remaining. But the history of who they were has passed generations through generations.
  
  Humanity, the creatures who once ruled this world. In the far past, they were the only intelligent creatures, living on a spherical planet, for many reasons considered strange, they were born naturally surrounded by no magic nor mana.
  
  They were intelligent but they also were powerless. As generations passed their cities grew and relied on the technology and knowledge they had collected. Their race never met the beauty of magic, yet it prospered. stories say they were able to explain the workings of the world through numbers and symbols. They were able to touch other planets without magic which could protect them from the void of space.
  
  Their world was a set of perfectly made calculations that gave them every answer, on the realm of the laws of the universe, even what they lacked the believe and knowledge of the existence of magic or mana as we know it.
  
  That would change, the day this little planet roaming scattered in the immense space was discovered by the gods. They were impressed by their achievements, they just made what seemed impossible, a civilization that didn’t rely on gods for their crops to grow or for diseases to disappear. They wanted to reward them for their resilience and their ability to get over their great limitations.
  
  And so the gods created new beings to serve and introduce humanity to the magic, creatures inspired by their legends and religions. They called us angels.
- ## Chapter 3: The Angels
  
  Since the first moment of our creation, our purpose was clear, we would take care and serve humanity. We would use our power to help them progress as a species now with the aid of magic and we would live in harmony with them.
  
  It was a law we were physically and mentally unable to break. No matter how much we would want to try. Just they thought of it caused extreme pain.
  
  Humans received the first of our kind with celebration and curiosity. Even skeptical people respected us and our power, they were intrigued by it. We were treated as equals or even superiors, by those who followed a religion of an old mythological god.
  
  With us, a new era arrived, where magic and technology were combined. Humanity had a curiosity, resilience and experience we lacked, and we had the mana and the power humanity was never given for their purely evolutionary birth. It was something close to perfection. Until it just wasn’t.
- ## Chapter 4: war
  
  The truth is, even without magic, humans were powerful, very powerful. And that power if not used to bring this civilization to one of the most advanced that may exist out there. It was a scary force able to bring destruction its way, with weapons so powerful and complex we might never truly understand, not anymore.
  
  Among the humans who received our species, there were the fearful, the envious, the power-lust, and those who could not accept that two species could live together, combine that with people with the destructive power of humanity, and peace was never meant to last.
  
  It was called a war, but it was a massacre, chained to the orders of the gods, our ancestors were unable to fight back despite their power, whole cities burned down and angelic bodies filled the streets in bloody inquisition.
  
  Humans themselves weren’t safe from the bloodshed, with even more wars growing among them for a multitude of reasons, economic failure, human genocide, and people using the chaos to do the worst things imaginable.
  
  Angels almost went extinct with small groups hiding away just waiting to be found. They prayed to the gods to save them, to have mercy for them, begging under the sun and moon to be saved. No one seemed to hear.
- ## Chapter 5: The Fallen
  
  And no one heard, or if they did, the gods did nothing. More and more angels were killed, and the survivors were pushed to an extreme where they had no option but to surrender and accept their destiny.
  
  But one just couldn’t, legends talk of one angel, one so desperate for survival, did the only thing that gods would have never approved, they sold their soul to a dark dangerous power, to break the orders the gods imposed. Falling from grace to not die.
  
  The power they gained was incomparable to anything else. They became an horrible monster with black wings and golden eyes, amorphous monsters with no will but to end humanity. After them, another six angels fell, all different but all with the same bloodlust.
  
  In a matter of days, their sole power was enough to turn the tide of war. No human weapon was able to withstand their unchained power. And the ruins of human cities were the sole thing to be found.
  
  Technical speaking, angels won the war, but there was no celebration, darkness covered the ruins of civilization and even angels had to hide in fear from the monsters the war created. No more deaths happened on their side, but no one knew what would happen the moment the last human died, if their lust would end. Or if might also be their end.
  
  Chapter 7: the end of the world
  
  But they didn't even have to wait to know. As only a few small groups of humans were left, a light filled the sky. They gods appeared once more, but not to save them. Their coming marked an attack to eradicate the fallen.
  
  A flash of light filled the sky, and everything was crumbling. Huge chunks of islands that once med continents, floating into space, as the very core of the planet stopped working. Soon enough, nothing was left. No life on Earth. could survive in the void space. It was just the nyphen and maybe a few little micro-genes more versatile to survive.
  
  They were alone, and the gods were gone once again. And the planet crumbled into space. But then everything stopped. The pieces of Earth returned, moving by some form of magic force unknown to this day, to new places around a core of mana forming inside what once was planet Earth. Nyphen didn't know what to do. The attack destroyed pretty much everything, nor they homes, nor the human cities survived, everything became a cold wasteland. And the mana core of the planet barely emitted enough heat to keep the crumbled world with arctic temperatures.
  
  Earth was gone, this was the end of the world. Any human left after the fallen succumbed after the gods attack and nyphen were left alone to pick up the pieces and rebuild their civilization.
  
  No one knows exactly how the name came. That was an event lost in story. But somewhere among the years. This reborn world was renamed.
  
  And its new name was Vielora.
